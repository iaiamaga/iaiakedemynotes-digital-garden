**Business logic** is the part of a software program that encodes real-world business rules, determining how data is created, stored, modified, and processed to achieve specific organizational objectives.  It acts as the intermediary between the user interface and the database, handling calculations, validation, and workflow automation while remaining distinct from technical infrastructure or presentation code. 

It is **important** in software development because it ensures **consistency**, **efficiency**, and **data integrity** across all transactions and interactions.  By automating routine tasks and enforcing policies, business logic reduces manual work, minimizes errors, and allows applications to scale and adapt to changing business requirements without disrupting the underlying technical architecture. 

Key aspects of business logic include:

- **Rule Implementation**: Translating formal business policies (e.g., tax rates, loan eligibility) into executable code.
    
- **Workflow Orchestration**: Defining the sequence of operations and conditions for processes like order processing or user authentication.
    
- **Separation of Concerns**: Keeping business rules separate from UI and data access layers to improve maintainability and scalability.
