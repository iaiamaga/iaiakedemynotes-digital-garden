**SHEFI GRADUATION!!!!!!!!!!!!!**

Seriously — we even had a DJ party in the metaverse!!  
Such a high-energy way to close this season.

It wasn’t just about learning Web3, but learning alongside a group of women who are forward-thinking, determined, and unafraid to confidently take up space. That inspires me a lot, and I find myself becoming more and more motivated and confident to explore and actively contribute to Web3. I’d really love to connect with more SheFiers!

I also want to take a moment to express my gratitude to shefi.org, Maggie Love, and the sponsors (Base, Trezor, Rootstock Collective, Decentraland, CoinFella) for the opportunity to be part of SheFi Season 16 and for the scholarship. And if I’m here today, it’s also thanks to the welcoming SheFi Brasil community — much love to Brazil!!

It was an honor and a truly valuable opportunity to learn from Maggie and the SheFi community about tools, real experiences from women in Web3, and core concepts like staking, yield, liquidity pools (something I used to struggle with and now understand), stablecoins, how to better use AI, and much more.

Throughout the program, I noticed a shift in my perspective. Things like the financial market — which once felt overwhelming and intimidating — started to feel much more approachable. SheFi, especially Maggie Love’s sessions, helped me realize that it’s not as scary as it seems. That gave me more confidence to dive deeper, especially into the areas that challenge me the most — because that’s where real growth happens.

On top of that, there were many valuable insights on decision-making, common pitfalls, and consistency (especially when motivation fluctuates). With each session, I found myself becoming more motivated.

The classes were great, and the energy always made me want to show up and participate. Maggie Love and Syd created such a welcoming environment, with truly incredible organization and support. Syd was an exceptional co-host — congratulations and thank you!!!!!

On top of everything, attending a party inside Decentraland was a whole experience on its own… super fun — and a bit chaotic at times since I was using my phone hahaha.

I also want to thank SheFi Brasil for guiding me to SheFi. Without this Brazilian community, I would feel much more distant and lost in this ecosystem. But now I truly feel like I’m making progress. More importantly, I feel surrounded by a vibrant and welcoming community. In the near future, I hope to join more meetups and become increasingly active.

I’m closing this first SheFi Scholar cycle with more clarity, direction, and an even stronger desire to keep going!
